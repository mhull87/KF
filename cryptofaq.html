What is blockchain technology?

Turns out a blockchain isn't so mysterious. Technically, your hard disk is a blockchain, and every file on it is DEFINITELY a blockchain. The thing that makes cryptocurrency blockchain special is the unspoken adjective “network-distributed” before blockchain. Think of a file with its blocks scattered around the net, with a file system that knows where they are and can reassemble them into a useable file. That’s almost it (that’s actually more like a bit torrent). Now think of such a file that *can be updated”, and now we’re getting somewhere. Toss in some encryption to protect the nature of the activity, and now you have a blockchain! Such a blockchain doesn’t have to be used for finance, but the transactional nature of block structures pretty much implies that it could be ideal, and it might just be ideal. That’s it! Like many such explanations it's a little oversimplified, but I hope it gives you a basic understanding of the data structure.
